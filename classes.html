Coursework

A selection of courses that have shaped my understanding of aerospace engineering, mathematics and control systems. Only the most relevant courses are listed here.

Penn State (First Year)

Linear Algebra

Fall 2023

Fundamentals of vector spaces, matrices, eigenvalues and eigenvectors. Provided the mathematical backbone for later courses in numerical methods and control theory.

Electricity & Magnetism

Spring 2024

Covered electrostatics, magnetic fields and Maxwell’s equations. We analysed circuits, waves and electromagnetic interactions through problem‑based labs.

Capstone Project

Spring 2023

Led a team project tackling a real‑world engineering challenge. Our design won a departmental prize for innovation and team collaboration.

Virginia Tech (Sophomore Year)

Electronics for AOE

Fall 2024

Analysed DC and AC circuits, phasors and frequency response. Lab sessions introduced instrumentation used in flight navigation and control systems.

Computational Methods

Fall 2024

Studied numerical algorithms for root finding, interpolation, regression and integration. Emphasised error analysis and MATLAB implementation.

Statics & Structures

Spring 2025

Examined equilibrium of rigid bodies, truss analysis, shear and moment diagrams and elementary stress–strain relations. These concepts underpin aircraft structures.

Dynamics

Spring 2025

Explored kinematics and kinetics of particles and rigid bodies using Newton’s laws, work–energy and impulse–momentum methods. Included planar and three‑dimensional motion.

Thin‑Walled Structures

Spring 2025

Analysed forces, deflections and shear flow in stiffened panels and multi‑cell beams. Important for wing and fuselage design.

Virginia Tech (Junior Year – In Progress)

Fluid Dynamics for AOE

Fall 2025

Introduces viscosity, vorticity, conservation laws and boundary layer theory. Applies Navier–Stokes equations to airfoils and wings.

System Dynamics & Control

Fall 2025

Covers linear time‑invariant systems, vibration of mechanical systems and PID control design. Projects involve simulating and tuning controllers in MATLAB/Simulink.

Aerospace Structures

Fall 2025

Investigates load paths in aircraft, stress transformation, buckling and plate bending. Provides tools for sizing components under combined loads.

Astromechanics

Fall 2025

Examines orbital mechanics including the two‑body problem, orbit determination and interplanetary transfers.

Nonlinear Dynamics & Chaos

Fall 2025

Studies phase‑plane analysis, limit cycles, bifurcations and chaotic behaviour. Highlights how linear control tools must be extended for complex systems.
